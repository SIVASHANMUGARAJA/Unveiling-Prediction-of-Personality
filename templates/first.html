Personality is the combination of qualities, traits, and behaviors that make a person unique, influencing how they think, feel, and interact with others.

ABOUT TRAITS

OPPENNESS

This dimension reflects the tendency to be open-minded, imaginative, and curious about the world. Individuals high in openness are often creative, adventurous, and willing to try new things. Those low in openness may be more conventional, practical, and prefer routine.

CONSCIENTIOUSNESS

Conscientiousness refers to the degree of organization, responsibility, and self-discipline a person has. High conscientious individuals are reliable, organized, and goal-oriented, while those low in conscientiousness may be more spontaneous, and less focused on planning.

EXTRAVERSION

Extraversion captures the level of sociability, assertiveness, and enthusiasm a person exhibits. Extraverts tend to be outgoing, energetic, and enjoy social interactions, whereas introverts are more reserved, reflective, and prefer solitude or smaller group settings.

AGREABLENESS

Agreeableness reflects the tendency to be compassionate, cooperative, and empathetic towards others. Individuals high in agreeableness are generally trusting, altruistic, and prioritize harmonious relationships, while those low in agreeableness may be more skeptical, competitive, or less concerned with others' welfare.

NEUROTICISM

Neuroticism refers to the degree of emotional instability, anxiety, and negative affect a person experiences. High neurotic individuals are prone to experiencing stress, worry, and mood swings, while low neurotic individuals are typically more emotionally stable, resilient, and able to cope with adversity effectively.
